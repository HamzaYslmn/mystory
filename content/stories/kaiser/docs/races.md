# Peoples

The sentient races, and how mana divides them. [Power System](power-system.md) sets the mechanics;
this page sets who has what. One rule sits under the whole balance: **no people is strong at
everything, and the stronger a people's magic, the fewer it is and the slower it breeds.** Power is
paid for in numbers.

Three axes decide a mortal's magic ([§5](power-system.md#5-what-separates-casters)): **capacity** (how
much mana the body holds, in Wh), **throughput** (how fast it pours out — a multiple of capacity, in
W), and **aptitude** (what the spark can be turned to). Efficiency — how well the mana converts — is
trained on top. And flesh caps the tap: **no mortal channels much past ~1 kW**
([§16](power-system.md#16-the-casters-limits)). The peoples just reach that ceiling by different roads,
or never reach it.

Capacity is **trained up off a racial base** ([§2](power-system.md#2-the-reservoir)): everyone starts at
their people's **base** (1×), drilling lifts a trained caster to about **2–3× base** and a **master** to
**5–10×**, where a born ceiling stops it — one or two prodigies a generation reach past 10×, no drill
further. So each race's base sets the whole ladder: a common human sits at his ~10 Wh base and a human
master at 50–100; an elf, drilled from birth, already stands at the trained tier, and its archmages reach
ten times a common elf's store.

The great equalizer stands behind all of them: a person's whole reserve is tiny — **~10 Wh for most,
~300 Wh for the rarest elf** — beside a single-use crystal measured in **kilowatt-hours**
([§18](power-system.md#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)). A mortal is *skilled but low-fuel*; a
crystal-beast is *crude but high-fuel*. A dwarf out-finesses a blood-wolf, and the wolf still
out-powers him thousands of times over on its purple stone. This is why magic is a craft, not a
war-winner, and why beasts and crystals decide battles that mortals cannot.

Fields below: **What · Homeland · Lifespan · Numbers · Mana · Signature · The trade** (the ceiling and
the floor that keep the people balanced).

## Demographic rules

**Lifespan is not population.** The lifespan below is a biological upper range for a healthy adult,
not life expectancy at birth. Population follows five separate variables: age at maturity, mean age
of parents, birth interval, children who survive to adulthood, and violent/disease mortality. Long
life slows replacement only when maturity and generation time also lengthen.

| People | Adult at | Typical first child | Generation | Children reaching adulthood | Population consequence |
|--------|----------|---------------------|------------|-----------------------------|------------------------|
| Elves | 40–60 | 80–130 | 100–150 years | ~2.0–2.2 | almost stationary; a war is remembered for centuries |
| Dwarves | 25–35 | 40–60 | 55–75 years | ~2.2–2.7 | slow recovery, but not as brittle as elves |
| Humans | 15–18 | 20–25 | 25–30 years | ~2.5–3.5 | fast replacement and dense farming settlements |
| Beastmen | 12–14 | 16–20 | 18–22 years | ~3.5–5 | fastest turnover; high hunt and feud mortality consumes the surplus |

These are **survivors to adulthood**, not total births. Famine, disease, war, and class change the
real figure. Revise a number only together with the population rank it supports.

**Longevity compounds knowledge and wealth.** An elf cannot be allowed five centuries of unlimited
disciplines, interest, and foreign landholding without quietly owning the world. Minds have
**interference**: most people sustain mastery in only **two or three disciplines**; learning more
remains possible, but old patterns lose speed and efficiency. Elven property outside the West is held
by clan or embassy and expires with the treaty that grants it; it cannot accumulate forever as
personal foreign debt and land. Dwarven workshops likewise belong to houses and guilds, not an
immortal individual balance sheet.

## Elves — the deep reservoir

- What: ancient, few, long-lived; every elf a born caster
- Homeland: the West
- Lifespan: ~500-year biological ceiling; adulthood comes late, and violence or disease can end one far sooner
- Numbers: the smallest of the peoples — not because longevity itself prevents births, but because a
  100–150-year generation and near-replacement fertility make every death slow to replace
- Mana: **all** elves cast — mana-drill begins in childhood, so every elf already stands at the trained
  tier off a high **base (~30 Wh)**. Most hold **50–100 Wh**; about **one in a hundred** masters reach
  **100–200 Wh**, and **one in a thousand** the **300 Wh** archmage ceiling — ten times a base, with the
  finest efficiency in the world. Throughput stays unhurried: ~1× common, up to ~3× trained. So an elite
  elf pours ~**900 W** near the mortal ceiling for ~20 minutes, or ~**300 W for about an hour**, before
  the 300 Wh reserve runs dry and a day's rest refills it
- Signature: sustained, refined battle-magic and enchantment — the best patterns and circuits made, and
  held the longest
- The trade: supreme at magic, and almost no one to wield it. Isolationist, thin on the ground, slow to
  recover a single loss. Their interference limit prevents five centuries from making every elf a
  master of every discipline. A handful of true archmages, never an army

## Dwarves — the burst and the forge

- What: stout, long-lived master-smiths; small reserves, ferocious output
- Homeland: the North
- Lifespan: ~250 years
- Numbers: few, but more than the elves
- Mana: dwarves take to the craft easily, so most learn it. A common dwarf holds only **~10 Wh** (his
  base) — a trained smith 20–30, a master 50–100, and the strongest smith alive the rare **200 Wh**
  prodigy. But the reservoir isn't the point: their gift is **throughput**. That 200 Wh master throws at
  **5× — a full ~1 kW**, the hardest burst any mortal makes. They do not sustain; they **spike**
- Signature: **body-enhancement and impact weapons** ([Projection](power-system.md#projection)). A
  scribed hammer or axe buffers a charge between swings and dumps it on contact — a master lands
  **1–2 kJ a blow**, enough to split a shield. Their **ground-set impact shield** doesn't absorb a blow
  (absorbing is dear, [§13](power-system.md#negation)) — it **turns the strike's vector down into the
  earth**, so the ground takes the reaction and only a fraction reaches the dwarf. And no one builds
  crystal **devices** better: the dwarves arm the world
- The trade: kings of burst and craft. The **common** dwarf is capped at a candle's worth of stored
  mana; the exceptional 200 Wh master is rare and can throttle down, but dwarven training and doctrine
  optimize decisive bursts rather than long shaping. Drain the reserve and the hammer is just iron.
  Mountain cities import grain and cloth; device and metal exports pay for food, making blockade their
  strategic weakness

## Humans — the balanced many

- What: short-lived, adaptable, everywhere; masters of nothing, adequate at all
- Homeland: all of it — humans are the connective tissue between the others
- Lifespan: ~70–80 years
- Numbers: vast and the **densest settled** people — cereal farming, roads, and towns put more humans
  on each cultivated valley than any other people. A war's losses are a generation, not a century
- Mana: humans are farmers with no one to teach them, so magic mostly goes unlearned — only about
  **one in five** ever picks it up, usually a trick or two off a **wandering mage** passing through:
  starting a fire, a few heartbeats of light. Those dabblers carry the human **base ~10 Wh** and output
  about as much (~1×, ~10 W). A properly trained caster reaches 20–30 Wh, a master 50–100 — the trained
  few who actually matter. And once in a generation a human is a **universal engineer** at ~**100 Wh**,
  the equal of a dwarf master at the workbench — never more than a handful alive
- Signature: **arrow-acceleration and body-enhancement** in war; **engineering** in craft. No single
  peak, every field entered
- The trade: outdone in magic by elves, at the forge by dwarves, in the body by beastmen — and
  everywhere, numerous, adaptable, quick to recover. Ubiquity is the human weapon. (Kaiser lands here:
  the rare engineer, in a body with a hidden [Mist bond](power-system.md#20-ocak-kanlı))

## Beastmen — the strong hand

- What: cat- and dog-kin — human-shaped, with ears, tails, and beast-quick bodies
- Homeland: the East, an enormous belt of forest, steppe, and river country; they live with the land,
  not over it
- Lifespan: ~50–60 years
- Numbers: the most numerous by total head-count only because the East is by far the largest inhabited
  territory and their generation is shortest; humans remain much denser and more urban
- Mana: **low, and body-enhancement only.** A beastman carries little mana — a **~5 Wh base**, ~**15 Wh**
  at the strongest — enough to **boost his own body** (a harder hit, a faster dash). They **learn it young,
  at the hunt**, passed down as a hunter's skill rather than scribed from a book. The reservoir was never
  their gift: there are no beastman capacity-masters, only stronger bodies. Layered on a frame already
  stronger and faster than a man's, that little mana makes a fearsome close fighter. But it is the whole of
  their magic: they don't scribe circuits or build devices
- Signature: **enhanced body** — natural strength and speed, pushed further by self-enhancement learned
  in the chase. What another race must build entirely out of mana, a beastman mostly already has and
  only tops up
- Economy: mixed horticulture, grain patches, herds, and hunting support the population; pure hunting
  could never feed their numbers. Villages do ordinary smithing, leatherwork, and carpentry. What they
  lack is the long institutional chain for **mana-circuit** craft, so they buy dwarf-made magical
  blades and tools
- The trade: the strongest bodies and the largest numbers — and it stops at the body. Split into small
  feuding villages with few circuit teachers and no unity, strength rarely gathers into state power

## Ocak Kanlı — the Mist-mark across peoples

Not a people and not a simple Mendelian bloodline, but a **rare Mist-mark** that can surface in any of
them ([§20](power-system.md#20-ocak-kanlı)): a direct channel to the Mist, born once in a great while,
with only a weak and unreliable family tendency. This avoids requiring all peoples to be one
interbreeding species. **Kaiser is secretly one** — he doesn't know it, and the reveal is far off.

## Synthora — the colony that sings

A **separate species**, not one of the four peoples: a soft-bodied colonial organism between **octopus
and slime**, working through octopus-like **tentacles** instead of limbs. It is the one people that turned
**sound** into power.

- What: a gel-and-tentacle colonial organism, **herbivorous and unaggressive** — but it **guards its
  ground absolutely**: no outsider enters a Synthora holding and leaves
- Homeland: a **handful of sites** in all the world, almost always beside an **echo-crystal cave**
- Reproduction: **budding** — a colony grows new individuals from itself
- Body & king: individuals cluster into working **bodies** of about **12**, each a tentacled shape that
  grips tools and instruments. A colony's **king** is a single vast fused mass of some **500** individuals
  — immobile, the seat of its greatest song
- Voice: a Synthora has **no vocal cords** but **hears** keenly. At adolescence each is given an **echo
  crystal** — its instrument and its voice; only then can it make its own music and join a body or the
  colony's chorus. Take the crystal and it is mute
- Affinity: **sound, at ~100%** ([racial affinity](power-system.md#casting-methods)) — masters of
  **music, sonic mimicry, and instrument-craft**, casting the Sound discipline with no waste where the
  best circuit reaches ~80%
- Mana: each individual is born with only ~**5 W**, the feeblest caster alive. The power is in **many**,
  not one. Massed casters everywhere else **beat out of phase and clash** (crystal banks explode this way,
  [§11](power-system.md#11-sharing--combining-mana)); the Synthora **solved it** — echo-crystal music
  **phase-locks** every member's separate cast so the *effects* superpose cleanly. No mana crosses between
  bodies (the [closed body](power-system.md#6-the-three-laws) still holds) — each spends only its own, yet
  a hundred small voices sing as one. So a 12-body pushes ~**60 W**, and a 500-strong **king ~2.5 kW** of
  coherent sound, past the ~1 kW that stops any single mortal — reached by numbers, not a bigger tank
- The trade: **feeble alone, formidable together, and only at sound.** The great capacity lives in the
  king, which cannot move; the colony is few, scattered, and purely **defensive**; and all of it is sound
  and nothing else. A people to bargain with, seldom to fight
