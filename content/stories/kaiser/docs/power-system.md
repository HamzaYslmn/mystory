# Power System — Mana Engineering

> **Canon.** Power is an internal skill — learn to *feel* mana, then spend it — and every effect
> obeys **real physics** on a **Watt-hour budget**; better physics is the edge. The system is
> **fully physical**: no metaphysical tier, no mind or soul magic. Three laws
> ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)) close the usual exploits. The old rune-crystal *system* is retired;
> crystals survive only as **mana storage** ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)). Writing:
> [Engineering Doctrine](engineering-doctrine.md); reveal pacing: [Narrative Rules](narrative-rules.md).

# Part I — Fundamentals

## 1. What mana is

Mana is **energy** — real, conserved, measured in Watt-hours. Every feat of magic is a budget you can
compute; every limit here falls out of that.

- **From the Mist.** Mana wells from the Mist, gathers in living things, and returns to it when they
  die.
- **Everywhere, uneven.** The universe is soaked in mana; only its **density** varies, and density
  drives regeneration ([§3](#3-regeneration)). It runs richest at a **Mana Well** (*Mana Kuyusu*), a
  few special regions, and a fresh **battlefield**, where spilled mana still hangs in the air.
- **Personal spin.** Each person's mana is polarized to them — a **spin** (*spin*) no two share — but
  it holds only **inside a living body**. The instant mana leaves the skin into open air it goes
  **spin-neutral**, indistinguishable from wild mana; a crystal's mana is neutral the same way. So
  spin can't pass between people, and a body fights a stranger's mana only where it is fed **straight
  in** ([§6](#6-the-three-laws), [§11](#11-sharing--combining-mana)).
- **It clings to the body.** Just outside the skin mana clings like a thin **gel**, a few centimeters
  deep; past about **5 cm** it loses hold, disperses into the surrounding region, and **raises that
  region's density** ([§3](#3-regeneration)). That gel is the whole reach of **touch** — the
  **contact law** ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)): mana moves only skin-to-skin, within the film.
- **Not directly measurable.** Like dark matter, mana is read only by what it does. To measure it,
  spend a known amount on a known task — boil a measured cup of water — and work back. (Kaiser does
  this, later.)
- **Conserved.** Mana is neither made nor destroyed. What you spend either converts to real energy —
  light, heat, motion — or spills as **raw mana** that neutralizes and **joins the local density**,
  drifting back toward the Mist over time. Waste is spilled mana, never stray heat. And because energy
  can't be erased, mana already in a body can't be cleared out — the basis of the third law
  ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)).
- **Chaotic where it pools.** Mana is not tame. Where the ambient density climbs far past even a Well's,
  it can **convert on its own, with no caster** — raw energy finding any release: rain boiled from a clear
  sky, the open air flash-frozen, the ground heaved into a new ridge overnight (force moving real rock,
  never matter from nothing — [§6](#6-the-three-laws)). These **mana-surges** are rare, local, and
  unpredictable, and the deepest pass into legend — the [Sky Islands](world-map.md) were born in one. What
  a caster does on purpose, wild mana now and then does by accident.

## 2. The reservoir

A person stores mana **in the body**.

- **Capacity is trained up off a racial base.** You are born into a people's **base** reserve; drilling
  lifts you to ~**2–3× base** trained and a **master** to ~**5–10×**, then a **racial-and-talent ceiling**
  stops it — one or two prodigies a generation reach past 10×, and no drill goes further
  ([§5](#5-what-separates-casters), [Peoples](races.md)). So a common human sits near his ~**10 Wh** base
  and a human master at 50–100; an elf, drilled from birth, already stands at the trained tier, and its
  archmages reach ~**300 Wh**. Efficiency and throughput keep improving past the ceiling; capacity does not.
- **The spark is common; using it is taught.** You live inside mana like a fish in water, but *feeling*
  and shaping it has to be learned, and who learns is set by people ([Peoples](races.md)): elves are
  drilled from birth, so every elf casts; dwarves take to it easily; beastmen pick it up hunting. Humans
  are the exception — farmers with no one to teach them, so only about **one in five** ever learns even a
  trick, and most of those know only lamp-light or fire-starting. A ~10 Wh trickle lights a lamp, not a
  battlefield; the casters who *matter* are the trained few with the capacity and skill to back the spark
  ([§17](#17-magic-in-daily-life)).
- **Running low tires you**, and running past empty is dangerous ([§16](#16-the-casters-limits)).

## 3. Regeneration

The reservoir refills **slowly**, and for almost everyone there is only one way: **live and rest**.

- **One reserve a day.** Food, drink, breath, and sleep carry a little Mist, and a fed, rested person
  refills their **whole reserve over about a day** (~**4% of capacity an hour**). The rate rides on the
  reserve's size, so a ~10 Wh human tops up in a day and a 300 Wh elf takes the same day to fill its far
  larger tank ([Peoples](races.md)). Richer ambient mana (a Mana Well) speeds it, poor country slows it.
- **Breathing techniques — trained.** A learned discipline lifts the pace to about **10% of capacity an
  hour**, a bit over double the resting rate, so a trained person recovers in **well under half the
  time**. And because self-[healing](#medical) runs on your own mana, faster refill means faster mending
  — a breather closes his wounds in roughly half the days. It is slow, focused work between fights, not
  power inside one.
- **Nothing mid-fight.** Even a breather gains only a tenth of a small reserve in an hour — nothing on a
  battle's timescale. Spend your reserve in a fight and you are spent until you rest; a drained mage is
  just a tired man. This is the quiet governor on the world: magic lights kitchens instead of leveling
  cities.
- **The fast paths — two only.** **Potions** ([Alchemy](#alchemy)) are refined Mist drunk straight — the
  quickest refill, and dear. A common restorative currently retails near **2 gold**
  ([Economics](economics.md)); its exact Wh-per-dose remains unset until a scene needs it
  ([§23](#23-open-threads)). And the
  **Ocak Kanlı** ([§20](#20-ocak-kanlı)) carry a **standing Mist-channel** that refills them on the move
  — the only mortals who need not stop to begin refilling, which is exactly what makes them rare and coveted.

## 4. How a cast works

Every cast, from lighting kindling to raising a golem, is the same four steps:

1. **Sense.** Feel the mana in your reservoir. Without this, nothing happens.
2. **Draw.** Pull it out at some rate — your **throughput** (*debi*). One caster pushes 100 W,
   another 10. Training widens the tap.
3. **Pattern.** Give the mana an instruction — what to become. A crude mental image, a built mental
   construct, or a circuit scribed into an object: the three **methods** of Part II.
4. **Convert.** Mana runs through the pattern into real energy. **How much converts is the pattern's
   quality** — your **efficiency** (*verim*).

**Efficiency belongs to the pattern, not the mana.** A crude image is a leaky mold: most mana pours
past and disperses. A novice converts ~**1%** — of 100 Wh, 1 Wh becomes effect and 99 drift back to
the Mist. Tightening that mold is the master's whole art, so **two casters with equal reserves can
differ a hundredfold** in what they do. The material never converts or adds energy — a matched
substrate only **reduces avoidable loss** for a chosen output, while a wrong one leaks or breaks
([§9](#9-magical-circuit)).

## 5. What separates casters

| Axis | Innate / learned | Meaning |
|------|------------------|---------|
| **Capacity** | born ceiling, trained toward it | How much mana the body holds. Training grows it off a racial **base** — ~2–3× trained, 5–10× master — **up to a racial-and-talent ceiling** (~10 Wh common human, ~300 archmage elf; [Peoples](races.md)); past the ceiling nothing lifts it. |
| **Efficiency** (*verim*) | learned | What fraction of spent mana becomes effect. Starts ~1%. **The master's real lever.** |
| **Throughput** (*debi*) | learned | Instantaneous draw rate — one caster sustains 100 W, another 10 W. |
| **Regeneration rate** | innate + trained | How fast the reservoir refills. Base: whole reserve over ~a day (~4%/h). A trained **breathing** technique roughly doubles that (~10%/h, halving recovery); only **potions** and the **Ocak Kanlı** channel are faster ([§3](#3-regeneration)). |
| **Learning speed** | innate | How fast a discipline yields to study. |

**Capacity is a wall, efficiency is a door.** Training fills the reserve to its born ceiling and stops;
from there only efficiency keeps opening. A lazy caster with a big reserve still loses to a disciplined
one who converts forty times as much of a smaller store — the open-ended growth is craftsmanship, not a
bigger tank.

**Throughput tiers.** Draw is a multiple of capacity, and the multiple is set by **people and training**
([Peoples](races.md)): a **novice** pushes about **half his capacity in watts**, a **trained** caster
about his **full capacity**, and **burst-specialist peoples (dwarves) up to ~5×**. But flesh caps the
tap: **no mortal channels much past ~1 kW** ([§16](#16-the-casters-limits)) — a dwarf master reaches it
from a 200 Wh reserve at 5×, an elf elite from 300 Wh at ~3×, and most people never come near. Capacity
is the wall; this is how fast you can throw it.

## 6. The three laws

The hard locks that keep the system honest. Every "why doesn't someone just…" hits one of these.

**1. Contact.** Mana moves only where things touch — skin to skin, or through the thin gel that clings
within ~5 cm of the body ([§1](#1-what-mana-is)). It can't cross open air, so you can't push mana into
a distant target. What crosses distance is the **energy it becomes** — convert it to heat, light, or
force at your hand, and that travels by ordinary physics. The one bridge is a **Bornmülleriana resin**
cable, which conducts mana along its length by unbroken contact ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy))
— the world's only mana wire.

**2. Conservation.** Mana is neither made nor destroyed ([§1](#1-what-mana-is)). No device outputs
more than it is fed; one that seems to amplify is **trading** — force for distance, speed for torque —
never making energy. No perpetual motion.

**3. The closed body.** A living body is already full of its owner's spin-locked mana. To run a
hostile pattern inside it you would first have to clear that mana out — erase energy — which
conservation forbids. So **no cast reaches into a living body to harm it**: no boiling blood, no
stopping hearts. Harm comes the ordinary way — the stone, the fire, the broken ground. Only
**healing** reaches in, because it *adds* mana the body takes up and directs. But mana fed from
outside arrives spin-neutral, and the flesh must **re-spin it to its own polarity** before it can use
it — done to you by another, at healing speed, that forced conversion **burns like fire**. Only your
  own hand, feeding and directing in one act, heals you painlessly. Without a coherent Medical pattern
  the body reflexively rejects almost all incoming mana; the tiny accepted fraction burns, then the
  rest spills neutral. Forcing mana into an unwilling body is therefore **torture, never a scalable
  kill** — ordinary fire, force, and weapons remain the way magic harms flesh.

# Part II — Casting & sharing

## Casting methods

*How* you build the pattern ([§4](#4-how-a-cast-works)). Method is orthogonal to discipline — any
discipline, any method; the method sets **efficiency**.

| Method | Efficiency | One line |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| [Unstructured](#7-unstructured-casting) | ~1–10% (ceiling ~20%) | Crude image → effect. Everyone's everyday magic. |
| [Shaping](#8-shaping) | up to ~40% | A pattern built deliberately in the mind. Costs concentration. |
| [Magical circuit](#9-magical-circuit) | ~30–80% | A conductive-mana circuit drawn into an object. A craft. |

**Racial affinity — the one born exception.** A few **separate species** are tuned from birth to a single
domain — sound, heat, water, cold — and cast *that one thing* at ~**100%**, past every ceiling above;
everywhere else they cast like anyone. It is their **spin**, not their craft, so it never carries into a
circuit they scribe (that still caps ~80%). See [Peoples](races.md).

## 7. Unstructured casting

*Yapılandırılmamış.* Hold a rough image — *fire, there* — and push mana through it.

- **Who:** everyone who can feel mana; no schooling needed. The magic of daily life — kindling,
  house-lights, warming a pot.
- **Efficiency:** ~1% first try, ~5–10% with practice, ceiling ~**20%**. The rest spills as raw mana.
- **In a scene:** instant, casual, wasteful. An angry amateur empties himself in moments and barely
  marks a door.

## 8. Shaping

*Şekillendirme.* The caster **builds the pattern consciously** in the moment — aiming it, sizing it,
closing the leaks.

- **Efficiency:** up to ~**40%** — the working method of trained mages.
- **The price is concentration.** A novice must stand still and silent; one spoken sentence collapses
  it. A veteran shapes while moving, even fighting, but a broken focus still spills the cast — pain,
  shock, a shout at the right moment.
- **In a scene:** the shaper's stillness before the effect *is* the tension.

## 9. Magical circuit

*Büyü Devresi.* The pattern is drawn **into an object** as a permanent **mana circuit** — a trace of
conductive **mana-ink** ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)). Mana poured through runs the
circuit and converts at its quality every time, no concentration needed. The circuit *is* the pattern:
it does the conversion — **no catalyst material is required** ([§4](#4-how-a-cast-works)).

- **Efficiency:** ~**30–80%** — the high road, but only if the circuit is good. Even the finest sealed
  *kozak* ink tops out near **80%**: the resin conducts but has real **resistance**
  ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)), so nothing scribed reaches 100%.
- **Losses are like wire resistance.** A broken, thin, or smeared trace leaks; a **longer** circuit
  loses more along its length — and, like resistance, also caps **throughput**
  ([§5](#5-what-separates-casters)). All of it spills as **raw mana**, never heat ([§1](#1-what-mana-is)).
- **Mana-ink.** **Spent, neutral crystal powder** worked into **Bornmülleriana resin**. The resin is the
  **conductor**; the depleted powder controls geometry, holds an edge, and gives a stable surface for
  the pattern, but supplies no charge and is not consumed as fuel
  ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)). Fresh charged crystal is never ground into a
  permanent trace. Ink grade — set by resin purity, grain, and sealing — sets how near the ~**80%**
  ceiling a circuit can get; the resin's resistance also means a **longer** trace loses more. The
  surrounding varnish or substrate is the **insulator**, never the resin itself.
- **The substrate must make sense.** It has to be **solid** (a trace won't hold on sand), and it must
  **survive the output** — a heat circuit on wood burns the wood, a force circuit cracks a brittle
  block, an electric circuit shorts a conductor. A **matched** substrate raises that output's
  efficiency: **perovskite** under a light circuit, refractory under heat, hard dense stone under
  force. This is all the old "transducer material" ever was — an *optional loss-reducer and a survival
  constraint*, never an energy amplifier or a required key. Total circuit efficiency still cannot
  exceed the resin-limited ~80% ceiling.
- **A craft, learned as one** — [Spell Formula](#spell-formula), years of it. A sloppy circuit (~30%)
  loses to a good [shaper](#8-shaping) (~40%); it wins only by being **permanent and needing no
  concentration**. A bad circuit on the wrong substrate is a grenade with a handle.

## 10. Magical devices

What circuits build: a real machine of **traces, switches, valves, and ordinary mechanisms** that runs its scribed circuit off a
socketed **single-use crystal** ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)). No caster need stand
over it — the crystal feeds the circuit until it is spent, then a fresh one drops in, like changing a
battery. This is how the **many who never learned to cast** ([§17](#17-magic-in-daily-life)) use magic at all.

*Example:* a 250-lumen magical lamp needs ~0.83 W of optical output; a 30–80% scribed circuit runs it
for roughly **15–40 continuous days** from a blue crystal (~1 kWh) before it is
spent. A city's luxury quarters burn through crystals to stay lit all night.

## 11. Sharing & combining mana

How mana moves between people and things, all under **contact** ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)) and **spin**
([§1](#1-what-mana-is)).

- **No reserve transfer person to person.** Two spins clash; neutral mana cannot become a second
  person's stored, freely spendable reserve. A healer can still **spend** mana through a Medical
  pattern that performs work immediately in the patient; that is an effect, not filling their tank.
- **Not into a crystal, either.** Crystals don't recharge — they are single-use cells you spend, not
  batteries you fill ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)). A mage can drive a device's
  circuit **directly**, hand on it, in place of a socketed crystal; but there is no pouring mana into
  storage to save or sell.
- **Healing another hurts.** A healer or device drives a controlled Medical pattern into a body not
  its own; it mends, but the flesh must re-spin the flood and that is **agony**
  ([Medical](#medical)). Without a valid healing pattern the body accepts almost none: pain and reflex
  rejection dump the feed back as raw mana. Forced feeding therefore cannot be scaled into a hidden
  kill spell. Only self-healing is painless.
- **Many can feed one work — in phase.** Rituals and group casting are real, but the casters must push
  **in phase**, their throughput timed together like AC. Out of phase the flows beat and cancel — the
  device melts, a seal chars. And the error **grows with the count**: a choir of a few dozen holds
  rhythm with **hymns and drums**, but thousands cannot — past some size the cast is likelier to **blow
  up and harm everyone around it** than to fire. That is the ceiling on army-scale magic, and part of
  why massed casters don't level the world.

# Part III — Disciplines

## 12. The discipline map

A discipline is *what you act on* — light, matter, the body. One grants nothing of another; each
takes **years**. They band by distance from hard physics:

- **Physical** ([§13](#13-physical-disciplines)) — applied physics on the world outside a living
  body. Kaiser's edge is a straight multiplier here.
- **The living frontier** ([§14](#14-the-living-frontier)) — the only reach to a living body:
  **Medical** (it adds), self-only **Enhancement**, and self-only **Transformation**. The closed-body law
  ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)) walls off the rest — no bodily attack, no mind magic.
- **Support crafts** ([§15](#15-support-crafts)) — professions built on the system.

Each entry: what it does · the physics · limits · in play.

## 13. Physical disciplines

### Projection

Emit raw energy: light, heat, sound, force, electricity.

- **Physics.** An energy transducer — 100 Wh = 360 kJ as whatever the pattern names:
  - *Light:* efficacy (lm/W) → lux × area × time.
  - *Heat:* Q = m·c·ΔT, plus loss to *hold* a temperature.
  - *Sound:* acoustic power → SPL (dB), inverse-square with distance.
  - *Force:* KE = ½mv² — and **recoil**: the third law shoves you back.
  - *Electricity:* P = V·I; ~10–100 mA across the heart kills; air arcs at ~3 MV/m. **Unknown in this
    world** — casters make light, heat, and motion directly and never thought to shape *current*; it
    is an untried output, Kaiser's alone ([§23](#23-open-threads)).
- **Limits.** Efficiency rules. The effect is born at your hand and travels as ordinary energy — you
  aim it, you don't place it.
- **Enhancing weapons.** The commonest battlefield use: pour kinetic energy into your own blade, spear,
  or arrow at the moment of use — KE = ½mv² added on top of the mundane strike (`kuvvet.py`). A plain
  arrow carries ~50 J; a boosted one can carry **kilojoules**, a heavy blow far more. Recoil still
  applies — the bow and the arm take the back-momentum ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)).
- **Chemical shortcut.** Mana-heat is dear (§21); *burning fuel* is far cheaper. A dragon sprays a
  flammable body-fluid and spends mana only to force air and sharpen the burn ([Bestiary](bestiary.md))
  — real combustion in open-air oxygen, not conjured heat. Kindling a fire beats conjuring one; the
  same logic (spend a little mana to unlock a lot of chemical or stored energy) is an edge Kaiser sees.
- **In play.** A lamp lights a cellar for hours off a sliver. ~70 kJ of force blows a barred door
  off — and throws you back a step.

### Alteration

Reshape **inert matter** — melt, freeze, harden, cut, bend, phase. Never a living body
([§6](#6-the-three-laws)).

- **Physics.** Pay the real bond, lattice, and phase energy in full:
  - Phase change: latent heat (water — fusion 334, vaporization 2257 kJ/kg).
  - Melt: c·ΔT to the melting point **plus** heat of fusion.
  - Cut / deform: yield stress × volume, or fracture energy per new surface.
- **Limits.** Permanent — the energy is spent and stays spent. Big masses, brutal bills; work the
  small load-bearing spot.
- **In play.** A smith heats an iron bar with no forge. A lock-bolt chilled brittle, then cracked
  with a tap — the lock, never the hand on it.

### Conjuration

**Temporary** shields and constructs.

- **Physics.** A held force field, or existing loose matter held in a temporary geometry. Making matter
  is out forever — E=mc² prices 1 g at ~**25 GWh**. Moving matter pays ordinary work (`force ×
  displacement`); maintaining the field then draws a defined **idle power in watts** set by area,
  stiffness, and leakage. `Force × time` is impulse, not energy, and is not used as a bill.
- **Limits.** A field costs every second and drops the instant you stop. Matter that was merely held
  falls; matter permanently bent, cut, or fused stays changed and belongs to [Alteration](#alteration),
  not Conjuration. Conjured things are rented.
- **In play.** A guard pulls loose cobbles into a held lip to break a charge; they collapse when his
  focus does. A duelist's force-disc holds a blade — and vanishes the moment his focus slips.

### Wards

Protect an area, person, or object.

- **Physics.** A barrier that **absorbs or deflects** energy and momentum; it costs what it counters —
  a plain arrow ~50 J, bullet ~1–3 kJ, fire a flux of W/m² × area × time.
- **Limits.** Deflection is far cheaper than absorption. And the threat is **not fixed** — fighters
  enhance their own weapons ([Projection](#projection)), so a boosted arrow or blade carries many times
  its mundane energy. A ward is really a **budget duel**: it holds only while its stored energy outlasts
  what the attacker pours into each blow. A ward sized for plain arrows dies to a boulder — or to one
  well-fed swing.
- **In play.** A caravan mage turns arrows all day on pocket change — but one boulder head-on empties
  him in seconds.

### Negation

Shut down another caster's working.

- **Physics.** You can't reach into a pattern from afar. Two ways to kill one: **overload its circuit**
  — pour more mana through a magical device than its trace can carry, and it burns out
  ([§9](#9-magical-circuit)) — or **counter the effect** with one of your own, cold against fire, a ward
  against force. No cheap magic-word dispel; you pay real energy either way.
- **Limits.** Overloading needs a way to feed the target — contact, its own supply line, or a resin
  cable ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)). Countering costs about what the effect costs.
  Useless against raw energy already in flight.
- **In play.** A saboteur back-feeds a ward-stone until its circuit chars; a mage smothers a fire-cast
  with a colder one of his own.

### Sensing (instruments)

There is no scrying — no mind that reaches into the air and simply *knows*. What a caster can do is
**build the instrument**: optics and converters that turn a signal the eye can't catch into one it can.

- **Physics.** Real signals only — heat (infrared), EM, vibration, trace chemistry, ambient mana
  density. The worked example is an **upconversion lens**: a coating that soaks up infrared and
  re-emits it as visible light, so a plain eye sees heat in the dark. Aperture sets resolution,
  signal-to-noise sets the floor; an instrument that lights its own target (active) pays ∝ range⁴,
  passive listening ∝ range². Mostly ordinary optics — mana only buys amplification.
- **Limits.** No trace, nothing to read; it discovers, never invents. And it is a **made thing** — it
  can be broken, fogged, blinded, or simply left at home. There is no innate sense to fall back on.
  Neutralized mana carries **no signature** ([§1](#1-what-mana-is)), so nothing reads *who* cast a
  spell — there is no spin forensics.
- **In play.** A hunter's infrared lens picks a warm body out of cold brush at night; a mana-density
  plate reads the dead patch a Bornmülleriana grove drains around itself
  ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)).

### Illusionism

Fake sights and sounds.

- **Physics.** Real light and sound, perception-tuned. The bill is matching the surroundings — noon
  (~100,000 lux) is costly, night nearly free.
- **Limits.** Fools eyes and ears, never touch. Light must scatter from something: a wall, cloth,
  smoke, mist, dust, or a carried scribed emitter. Clean open air cannot hold a free-floating image at
  a distance under the contact law. It also has tells — no warmth, wrong shadow, weak parallax.
  Quality is the budget spent hiding those.
- **In play.** A thief throws his image onto smoke at the far end of an alley to draw the guards —
  good at night, betrayed at noon by the wrong shadow.

### Animation

Semi-autonomous behavior in objects.

- **Physics.** Two bills:
  1. **Muscle** — actuator power per joint: τ = load × arm, power = τ·ω, summed × duty ÷ efficiency.
  2. **Coordination** — the "CPU": DOF × loop-rate × sensing. A simple repeating motion is nearly
     free; a balancing biped is costly and unstable.
  Plus a **structural ceiling** — the host must bear the joint torques or tear apart.
- **Limits.** No intelligence — it runs its written loop, nothing more. Complexity grows the CPU bill
  faster than the muscle.
- **In play.** A stone arm grinds grain all day, cheap and tireless. A war-golem balances only while
  its maker feeds the CPU, and staggers when his attention breaks.

### Combat

Fast casting under fire — always on the *surroundings*, never the body ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)).

- **Physics.** Applied Projection, Alteration, and Wards on a reaction-time budget. Speed trades
  against efficiency — no time to shape, so it burns hot.
- **Limits.** You can't touch the man, only his world. The winner spends less per exchange and turns
  ground, air, and loose matter into the weapon.
- **In play.** Not a bolt through the chest — flagstones erupt under his feet, a stone at lethal
  speed, a flash to blind, the air above him a wall of heat.

### Motive power — the mana-engine

How the world turns mana into **rotation**: not a spinning motor but a **push-pull engine**.

- **Physics.** A [force](#projection) circuit drives a piston one way, a return spring or a
  double-acting circuit the other — a solenoid-style reciprocator. A **crank and flywheel** turn that
  stroke into shaft rotation, exactly as a steam piston does. Continuous torque from a single material
  is out — spin needs a field and commutation, a whole machine; a reciprocator needs only a simple
  "push" pattern and ordinary mechanism.
- **Materials.** The mana side is a drawn circuit on a solid substrate ([§9](#9-magical-circuit)); the
  rest — piston, rod, crank, flywheel, bearings, spring — is ordinary metal and wood. The magic is the
  stroke; the rotation is machining.
- **Wear.** No waste-heat, so nothing melts ([§1](#1-what-mana-is)); it wears the **mechanical** way —
  bearings and crank fatigue, and the circuit erodes over millions of strokes like a worn cam, until
  it needs re-scribing. A machine that breaks and gets repaired — a mechanic's living.
- **Mechanical storage.** A raised weight banks shaft-work with negligible standing loss; a flywheel
  still bleeds through bearing drag and air resistance. Neither suffers a crystal's spontaneous mana
  decay ([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)), but both are bulky and costly. Water, wind,
  animals, and fuel beat crystal on bulk work; mana wins where portability, precision, silence, or
  immediate start is worth the premium.
- **In play.** A cart's wheel-hub is a small **assist**, brake, or emergency push-pull engine fed by a
  set crystal — a 100 W blue cell yields only ~36 W at the reference build, less than half a laborer's
  sustained mechanical output. Full propulsion needs an expensive kilowatt purple stone. The dead grey
  lines scribed into old wagon wood are its spent mana-ink circuit. The engineer's edge is double — a
  better circuit **and** a better mechanism (crank throw, flywheel, timing, compounding, gearing) — so
  the same Wh yields far more shaft-work than the local build.

## 14. The living frontier

The only domain that reaches a living body — and only to **add**, never to harm: Medical mends
another, Enhancement and Transformation remake only yourself. Everything hostile is closed by law
([§6](#6-the-three-laws)); no mind magic, no bodily attack.

### Medical

Diagnose and repair a body.

- **Physics.** An instrument or a probing trickle of your own mana ([§13](#sensing-instruments)) to
  find the fault, then **feed mana** the body takes up and directs. Energy is only half the bill: new
  flesh needs **matter**, and mana can't make it ([§6](#6-the-three-laws), E=mc²), so the body **spends
  its own biomass** — fat, water, nutrients — to build it. A starving man heals far slower, and heal too
  much and the patient wastes away feeding the mend. The energy runs ~5 kJ/g, so 100 Wh drives ~**72 g**
  at best. Plus a **rate limit** (heat and biology), an **information cost** (knowing the right
  structure), and the patient surviving it.
- **The pain.** Self-healing is painless; but mana from another arrives spin-neutral, and the body
  must **re-spin the flood** to use it — at healing speed that **burns like fire**
  ([§11](#11-sharing--combining-mana)). A patient screaming under a healer's hands is normal.
- **Limits.** Slow, exacting, hungry, and painful unless self-cast. Deep work needs scribed tools and
  hours. Self-mending is capped by your **refill** — a trained breather ([§3](#3-regeneration)) heals in
  about half the days.
- **In play.** A mage closes a cut on his own arm and feels only the drain; the same cut from the
  company healer has the soldier biting a strap.

### Enhancement

Drive your **own** body past its normal output — strength, speed, a hardened hide.

- **Self-only.** Like all body-reaching magic it works on **you** alone ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)),
  feeding mana into your own muscle and bone so they do more. Painless, like self-healing.
- **Physics.** Real work, paid every second you hold it: extra force at a joint is τ = load × arm,
  power = τ·ω. Living flesh takes **no circuit** — you can't scribe tissue — so a boost tops out at the
  **shaping** methods, never the high road, and its efficiency rides on the **mind** doing it: a brute
  on instinct manages only crude imaging (~**1%**), while an **intelligent** caster who truly shapes the
  boost reaches far higher — a **dragon ~50%**. And a hard **structural ceiling** — muscle can pull
  harder than tendon and bone will bear, so overreach **tears you apart** ([§16](#16-the-casters-limits)).
  The flesh also **tires**: a boost can't be held long or repeated without recovery, so even a full
  reserve can't spam it. Most beasts are 1% brutes that must carry a big **crystal** to afford one trick
  ([§19](#19-creatures)); the clever few are terrors.
- **The external answer.** Because innate boosting spills 99% of its mana, an **exoskeleton** — an
  [animated](#animation) frame you wear — wins big: it runs on a **scribed circuit (30–80%)** instead
  of flesh, turning the same Wh into **tens of times** more force. It costs capital to build and a
  coordination (CPU) bill to run, and it is bulky, but per Wh it dwarfs innate enhancement. That gap is
  the reason to build one — and Kaiser does, when he must.
- **In play.** A warrior doubles his strength for three heartbeats and pays for it; a caged beast's one
  gift is a killing burst of speed.

### Transformation

Remake a **living** form — only your **own**.

- **Self-only.** You command only mana of your own spin, and another body is closed to you
  ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)). You can remake nothing living but yourself.
- **Still legendary.** Even on yourself, three costs stack — the energy to move mass, the life-support
  to survive the change, the information to specify the new form. Miss one and you die mid-change.
- **In play.** A whispered legend of a mage who became something else — and the many who died halfway.

- **Kaiser's home craft.** Everything he is converges here.
- **In play.** The scribed perovskite lamp; a ward-stone over a gate long after its maker; a golem

### Alchemy

- **Feeds two systems:** [Regeneration](#3-regeneration) (potions) and [circuits](#9-magical-circuit)

- **In play.** A restoring potion after a fight; a salve for a wound; the perovskite stock a smith

# Part IV — The world

## 16. The caster's limits

The walls every caster lives inside:

- **Depletion.** A low reserve means fatigue; past empty the body pays from its **own energy** —
  shaking, cold sweat, tunnel vision — and pushing on, you faint, maybe die. Fainting is the body's
  breaker.
- **Concentration.** Shaping locks the mind ([§8](#8-shaping)); a shattered focus spills the cast.
  It is a resource enemies can attack.
- **Learning time.** Years per discipline, and no discipline grants another. No shortcut, no
  inheritance — a mage's disciplines are their life story.
- **The body's ceiling.** Flesh caps everything. Enhancement past what the skeleton bears breaks it;
  throughput past what the body channels — much past **~1 kW** — burns it. That ~1 kW is the hard mortal
  draw ceiling ([§5](#5-what-separates-casters)).
- **No harm to a living body** ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)) — every fight goes through the world between
  you, never the enemy directly.

## 17. Magic in daily life

From a village street:

- **Real casters are rare.** Whether anyone learns to cast is set by people ([Peoples](races.md)) —
  every elf does, only about one human in five — and even then a ~10 Wh trickle only lights a lamp. A
  caster who *matters* — who can fight, enchant, or scribe — is somebody: a village has a few, a city its
  guilds. This is the quiet reason the world isn't chaos: the spark is cheap, but the capacity and skill
  to throw fire are not.
- **But magic reaches everyone.** Even those who can't cast encounter scribed, crystal-fed devices —
  but ownership is a **luxury** ([Economics](economics.md)): a hand-lamp costs a floor worker months of cash wages, so
  the well-off keep private lamps and precision heaters, a village **shares** an emergency pump,
  clinic tool, or mana governor fitted to its watermill, and a tavern rents out light — while the poor
  still burn tallow candles and wood. Magic energy is a thing you buy, not a
  talent you need, but it is dear.
- **Mastery is rarer still.** Universities are few; most learn alone or under one teacher, and
  spellbooks cost a fortune. A trained shaper is somebody in a town, a circuit-scriber somebody in a
  city.
- **Premium energy, not bulk power.** Water, wind, wood, charcoal, people, and animals do the cheap
  continuous work. Crystals pay where portability, exact control, automation, healing, sensing, or
  combat justifies a price measured in days of labor per kWh. You cannot bottle and sell your own mana
  because crystals do not recharge, though casters sell on-site work. Rich households may own blue
  lamps; kilowatt purple stones belong to workshops, gates, expeditions, and nobles, not ordinary homes.

## 18. Storage crystals & the mana economy

**Single-use mana cells.** Small, heavy crystals that hold a fixed charge and spend it **once** — never
rechargeable, no filling. Burn one and it is done, like a lump of coal. And a crystal **slowly leaks even
on the shelf**, so a hoard bleeds away over the months whether you use it or not — which is why the trade
never slows: the world must keep mining and hunting just to stand still. This is what makes mana an
economy: energy is a consumable you buy, spend, and replace. And a crystal holds far more than a person —
a person's whole reserve is at most ~**0.1–0.3 kWh** (most hold a tenth of that), while even a small
crystal is measured in **kilowatt-hours**.

**Two kinds, two sources.**

- **Blue — mineral.** Mined or found in the ground, rare. Energy by volume: a **5 cm³ cube ≈ 1 kWh**.
  Its **output is low — a worked cell at most ~100 W**, enough to run daily devices (lamps, fans, small
  tools) but no more. Wiring many together to force more power **disrupts their frequency** the way massed
  casters clash ([§11](#11-sharing--combining-mana)) — too many in series or parallel beat out of phase
  and the bank can **explode**. So cheap mineral crystal cannot be stacked into a power plant.
- **Purple — biological.** Grown inside creatures steeped in high mana, dropped when the beast is killed
  ([§19](#19-creatures)). **Denser and far higher output** — this is what lets a dragon breathe a flood
  of fire or fly a huge body. Capacity varies with the beast; a **blood-wolf** ([Bestiary](bestiary.md))
  carries ~**30 kWh** at ~**2 kW** output — water-heater power, a rich household's choice. Big beasts
  carry fortunes.

**Size sets both.** A crystal's **output rises with its surface area**, its **capacity with its
volume** — so a bigger stone gives both more current and more charge, a simple *big stone, big power*
logic. A blood-wolf's fist-sized purple stone puts out ~2 kW; a dragon's is a boulder, and a fortune.

**Cutting trades charge for output.** Faceting a stone opens more surface, so a cut cell throws **more
power** than the whole lump did — but the working spills part of the stored charge, so you buy the higher
output with **lost capacity**. A scriber sizes the cut to the job: burst-power for a weapon, or the full
reserve left intact for a lamp meant to last.

**Raw vs. scribed.** Unworked, a crystal can deliver only a weak connected trickle (a few watts).
Disconnected shelf leakage is much slower — a good sealed cell loses roughly a few percent per month,
not a continuous multi-watt load — and grade, damage, and age change it. **Scribed** by
[Spell Formula](#spell-formula), it delivers up to its rated output —
~100 W blue, kilowatts purple — and a **cut, worked** cell higher still, at the cost of capacity (above).
Working a crystal is guild craft; the scribers sit at the center of the trade.

**The economy — coin and fuel.**

- **Currency.** 100 **bronze** (*tunç*) = 1 **silver** (*gümüş*); 10 silver = 1 **gold** (*altın*);
  100 gold = 1 **Magic Platinum** (*Büyülü Platin*), a cheque-like bearer-note carrying a magic seal
  that proves it genuine.
- **Prices have two axes.** Mineral crystal energy runs about **1 silver per certified remaining kWh**
  (a fresh 5 cm³ stone ≈ 1 kWh = 1 silver). Biological energy starts near **5 silver per remaining
  kWh** — a fresh ~30 kWh blood-wolf stone is ~**150 silver** (15 gold) before unusual output premiums.
  The certificate separately rates **energy (kWh), continuous output (kW), monthly leakage, and circuit
  grade**. Power, not just charge, is why purple commands a premium. Full cost-of-living in
  [Economics](economics.md).
- **Assay makes the market possible.** Mana cannot be read directly, so licensed assay houses combine
  mass/volume geometry, a calibrated brief discharge, leakage observation, and batch records; they
  seal the estimate and accept liability. An uncertified hunting stone sells at a deep discount. A
  beast that fought hard leaves a visibly valuable but economically disputed asset — ideal ground for
  fraud, guild power, and Kaiser's eventual instruments.
- **Two trades feed it.** **Miners** dig blue; **hunters** kill for purple. Both dangerous, neither
  farmable, and demand never stops — energy is consumed, so the world always needs more.
- **No storable personal-mana market.** You cannot bottle your own mana or recharge a crystal, and a person's whole
  daily refill is only his own small reserve ([§3](#3-regeneration)), worth a coin or two and gone
  keeping himself topped up. Nobody grows rich as a living battery. Everyone buys crystal fuel and burns
  it in devices. Casters still sell **services at the point of use** — healing, shaping, or directly
  driving a circuit — but the energy cannot be warehoused after they leave.

**Mana-ink & Bornmülleriana.** The resin is the world's one **workable mana conductor** — mana runs
along it by contact, like current down a wire ([§6](#6-the-three-laws)), but it has real **resistance**:
the longer the run, the more leaks, so a cable's efficiency **falls with distance** and even the finest
sealed *kozak* tops out near **80%**. Two uses: work powdered storage crystal into it to make
**mana-ink** and draw a [circuit](#9-magical-circuit); or run a length of raw resin as a **cable** to
feed a device from a distance, or to scribe onto one out of reach. It is **not a store** — it carries
mana, it does not bank it. (The one thing that beats it is a **unicorn's horn**, a rare near-perfect
conductor that passes mana with almost no loss — set as a staff or a weapon-haft, [Bestiary](bestiary.md).)
It comes from a tree that grows only beside a **Mana Well**,
**drinks mana** faster than ordinary country can feed, and so **cannot be farmed**: plant it in poor
air and it starves, and hand-feeding a plantation costs more than it ever yields (conservation,
[§6](#6-the-three-laws)). Extraction, never cultivation.

- **Harvested sealed.** Raw resin exposed to open air disperses its mana within moments
  ([§1](#1-what-mana-is)) and dies to dull gum, so the intact **cocoons** (*kozak*) are carried to a
  facility and locked in there. Field-tapped resin is the cheap, low grade; sealed *kozak* is the
  prize — and ink grade sets a circuit's efficiency ceiling.
- **Life banks two things.** Beasts grow **purple crystal** (the richest store), the Bornmülleriana
  grows **kozak resin** (the wire) — both Well-bound, monster-guarded, un-farmable, extraction-only.
  Mineral **blue crystal** is the third supply, dug from rare ground rather than grown.
- **The resin is the bottleneck.** A circuit needs crystal powder *and* resin. Crystal is only rare
  (blue mined, purple hunted); but resin comes only off the deadly ground around a **Well**
  ([§3](#3-regeneration), [§19](#19-creatures)) — which is why circuits and devices stay a
  guild-and-city craft, expensive, and the world is not paved with golems. **Northungard**
  ([§19](#19-creatures)), the one city on a Well, is the natural ink capital — and therefore regulates
  resin export, assay seals, and foreign workshops as instruments of state power. Transport leakage,
  monster pressure, and smuggling keep that monopoly strong but incomplete.

## 19. Creatures

Most creatures have no crystal and can't cast. One that does works magic on **instinct** — no thought,
reliable, one perfected trick flawless every time — almost always a single **physical-enhancement** on
its own body (speed, strength, a hard hide). The boost's efficiency rides on the beast's **mind**
([§14](#enhancement)): a brute on instinct manages only ~**1%**, so it gets away with its one trick only
because its **crystal** pays the bill. The rare **intelligent** beast is another matter — it *shapes*
like a mage (~**50%**) and need not stop at one trick; the **Special** monsters (a dragon,
[Bestiary](bestiary.md)) are exactly these, a handful in the world, and are deadlier for it. Crystal, or
wit, is what makes a crystal-bearer dangerous.

**The crystal is the fuel.** A beast's enhancement draws straight from its **purple crystal**
([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)) — single-use, so every leap and every gout of flame
**spends it for good**. Its body **buffers** the draw (like a caster's reserve,
[§2](#2-the-reservoir)), letting it spend in bursts far above the crystal's steady output, then pause
to refill. A quick kill leaves a full stone; a beast that fought long and hard leaves a half-drained
one worth far less — so drawn-out combat is an economic loss. Worked numbers per beast in the
[Bestiary](bestiary.md).

**Why you can't farm them.** A **purple** crystal grows only in **living tissue**, over **years**, in
**high ambient mana**. Cut out, it is a finished **single-use** cell
([§18](#18-storage-crystals--the-mana-economy)) — no more growth. And penning a creature loses money:
feeding a captive enough mana to grow one costs **more** than the crystal will ever hold (conservation
plus losses). Extraction beats husbandry — the trade runs on the hunt. (**Blue** mineral crystal has no
such origin; it is simply mined, and rarer than ore.)

**Why the hunt has law.** A valuable wild crystal is an open-access resource: without ownership and
limits, every hunter is paid to kill now and leave extinction to everyone else. Well-lords therefore
claim hunting rights, license expeditions, tax declared stones, close breeding seasons, and punish the
sale of juvenile cores. Guild quotas are imperfect and smuggling is lucrative. Monster danger slows
over-harvest; institutions are what keep it from becoming extermination.

**Mana Wells belong to monsters.** A Well is where the planet's core seeps mana to the surface: it
runs **densest in the first ~50 m**, almost unbearably rich, and its pull draws living things from as
far as **~50 km** around — so it is ringed by the deadliest beasts, thickest at its heart. (Magical
creatures turn up beyond that ring too, but rarely.) Cities rise *away* from the wells — the one
exception is **Northungard**, the northern capital, built on a Well only after a great war took the
ground from the creatures. This also kills any dream of *farming a Well* ([§3](#3-regeneration)): they
are rare, lethal, and monster-held, not real estate.

## 20. Ocak Kanlı

**Ocak Kanlı / Hearth-Blooded** — a rare cross-people **Mist-mark**, not a simple Mendelian bloodline,
with a **direct bond to the Mist**. Where everyone else
must eat, breathe, and rest a whole day to refill ([§3](#3-regeneration)), they pull mana **straight from
the Mist into the heart** — the only marked mortals with **standing regeneration**, refilling even mid-fight.

- **Numbers.** ~**+25 Wh/h** anywhere, needing no rich surroundings; trainable higher, up to ~**75 Wh/h**
  in a Well ([§3](#3-regeneration)) — many times what a full day's rest gives anyone else. A self-renewing
  supply that simply outlasts a foe who must stop and rest.
- **Vanishingly rare.** A handful in the world, so almost nothing is known — no school, no book.
- **Kaiser is secretly one.** He doesn't know; the reveal is much later. His bond is why the Mist
  carried him across and his spark caught instead of dissolving. Keep it subtextual — see
  [Characters](characters.md).

> **The engineer's edge in one line:** every effect is real physics, so knowing better physics is a

## 22. Worked formulas

**Light** (Unstructured, 5%): 100 Wh runs a 250-lumen lamp ~6 hours.
```
mana_Wh = 100 ; eff_pct = 5 ; lumen = 250
light_W = lumen / 300            # ~0.83 W for 250 lm
hours   = (mana_Wh * eff_pct / 100) / light_W   # ≈ 6
```

**Ward** (arrows absorbed — stopping energy ÷ per-hit energy, cf. `siper.py`):
```
mana_Wh = 100 ; eff = 0.4 ; arrow_J = 49        # ½·0.020·70²
absorbed_J = mana_Wh * 3600 * eff               # 144 kJ of stopping power
arrows     = absorbed_J / arrow_J               # ~2900 plain arrows (deflection stretches it further)
boosted_J  = arrow_J + 1*3600*0.2               # +1 Wh of KE at 20% -> ~770 J per arrow
boosted_n  = absorbed_J / boosted_J             # ~190 — enhanced weapons collapse the count
```

**Medical** (tissue, 100% efficiency → ~72 g):
```
mana_Wh = 100 ; eff = 1.0 ; e_tissue_kJ_g = 5
energy_kJ = mana_Wh * 3.6 * eff
tissue_g  = energy_kJ / e_tissue_kJ_g   # 72 g
```

**Ice** (naive dump vs. compressor cycle):
```
# naive: cool 20 K then freeze
c = 4.18 ; delta_T = 20 ; Lf = 334
Wh_per_kg = (c*delta_T + Lf) / 3.6          # ≈ 116 Wh/kg
water_kg  = 100 / Wh_per_kg                 # ≈ 0.86 kg

# compressor: coefficient of performance 3
ice_kg = 100 * 3 / 116                      # ≈ 2.6 kg
```

**Daily regeneration** (one whole reserve back in ~a day; scales with the reserve, so no surplus to sell):
```
reserve_Wh = 10                          # an ordinary human's tank
rate_Wh_h  = reserve_Wh / 24             # ≈ 0.42 Wh/h — one reserve per day
sellable   = rate_Wh_h * 24 - reserve_Wh # ≈ 0 — you refill yourself and no more
breath_Wh_h = 0.10 * reserve_Wh          # trained breathing ≈ 10%/h = 1 Wh/h
recover_h   = reserve_Wh / breath_Wh_h   # ≈ 10 h — well under half the day-long rest
```
