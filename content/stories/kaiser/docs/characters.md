# Characters

See also: [Premise & Themes](premise-and-themes.md) · [Power System](power-system.md).

## Kaiser — protagonist

- **Who he is.** A real engineer, not a movie hacker. Credentialed-adjacent skill wasted
  by the system. Maker, not fighter — his instrument is a tool, not a weapon.
- **His actual superpower — method *and* modern physics.** The new world's magic obeys real
  physical law, so his old-world engineering literacy **does** transfer and is a genuine edge:
  he knows a compressor beats direct cooling, that tissue costs real energy, what a budget of
  Watt-hours can and can't buy. On top of that runs the **diagnostic loop**: observe →
  hypothesize the failure mode → test cheaply → read the result → revise. Everyone around him
  works magic by rote on a pre-modern picture of the world; Kaiser reasons each effect from real
  physics and wrings efficiency out of it they can't. He *casts* like they do — but understanding
  is always upstream of the power. (This is the whole point of the
  [Engineering Doctrine](engineering-doctrine.md).) Its origin is now canon: old **Izzet**
  drilled this loop into him in the shop, years before the mana world (Ch2).
- **Flaw (for depth).** He trusts systems over people. He can read a machine in seconds
  but misreads humans — Anakara taught him to assume bad faith, and it costs him. His
  growth is learning that people are not badly-written code to route around. **Elin**
  cracks this open; **Ostragrad's** genuine fairness confuses him.
- **Hidden canon (Kaiser does NOT know this; reveal is much later).** Kaiser is actually
  an **Ocak Kanlı / Hearth-Blooded** — his body *channels* mana from the Mist on its own, a passive,
  standing regeneration **almost no one else has**: the Ocak Kanlı are a scant handful in all the world,
  born once in a great while into any people, and Kaiser is one. He has no idea. Keep it subtextual. It quietly
  explains why his compressed spark *ignited* instead of dissolving in the Mist (Ch4), and it
  is the eventual key to a self-renewing mana supply that lets him outlast people who must
  wait to refill. Do not tip this before he earns it.

## Arc 1 cast (old world)

### Izzet
Grumpy, exacting old master mechanic who runs the scrap-and-repair shop below Kaiser's flat.
Years ago he took a raw, undisciplined Kaiser under his wing and drilled the diagnostic method
into him — *"What is it doing? Not what you think — what is it actually doing?"* — so Kaiser's
whole survival skillset in the new world is, at root, Izzet's. He is the one person who
ever valued Kaiser and treated him as a person, not a statistic; his harshness at the bench and
his quiet generosity (the birthday meal, the coat, the heater left on) are the same care
wearing two faces (Ch2). Too proud to take help or even name the trouble, he keeps the shop open
— and a lit place for the boy to come back to — by borrowing from loan sharks, and means to
settle it himself; he never tells Kaiser, who only senses something is wrong and lets it go. When
the debt comes due they beat him down, and it is seeing Izzet hurt that drives the non-violent
Kaiser to his one act of agency, which gets Kaiser killed (Ch3). The relationship must carry real warmth — it is the only place Kaiser is
*seen*, and the whole emotional engine of Arc 1.

## On deck (established canon, enters Arc 2+)

### Elin
Orange-haired girl (~12) Kaiser saves in the forest and escorts to Ostragrad; speaks a
border dialect (Russian-adjacent), broken common tongue. Secretly **Lord Valerius's
daughter**. The human relationship that breaks Kaiser's system-over-people flaw.

### Lord Valerius
Elin's father; lord of Ostragrad, powerful northern noble. Spares Kaiser's life but does
not clear him; confines him to the city.

### Commander Vorn
Ostragrad garrison commander; experienced, calm, careful.

### Master smith — Boran
An exacting iron-and-circuit master; Kaiser is assigned to his workshop as a watched heavy
laborer. An ordinary mortal caster — his reserve refills only with rest, like everyone's.

### Captain Lyra
One of Valerius's commanders; distrusts Kaiser. Her **sword** carries scribed **impact** and
**edge/sharpness** circuits — it strikes harder and cuts cleaner than plain steel — and she herself
casts natural **self-enhancement** for speed. (Enhancement is self-only and can't be scribed into flesh
([§14](power-system.md#enhancement)); what is *etched* lives in the blade, not in her.)

### Toma
Arrogant journeyman in the workshop.

### Sena
Young soldier assigned to orient Kaiser in the city.
