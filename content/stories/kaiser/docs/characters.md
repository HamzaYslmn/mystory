# Characters

See also: [Premise & Themes](premise-and-themes.md) · [Power System](power-system.md).

## Kaiser — protagonist

- **Who he is.** A real engineer, not a movie hacker. Credentialed-adjacent skill wasted
  by the system. Maker, not fighter — his instrument is a tool, not a weapon.
- **Body (new world).** He died at **28**; the Mist put him back in his **24-year-old** body, and his
  memories reach only as far as 24 — the last four years went with everything else. Visibly de-aged,
  the lifetime of calluses thinned from his hands. He notices but can't explain it (Ch4). None of this
  is ever stated on the page: his shattered memory keeps no exact numbers, so in-scene he reads it off
  his own hands.
- **His actual superpower — method *and* modern physics.** The new world's magic obeys real
  physical law, so his old-world engineering literacy **does** transfer and is a genuine edge:
  he knows a compressor beats direct cooling, that tissue costs real energy, what a budget of
  Watt-hours can and can't buy. On top of that runs the **diagnostic loop**: observe →
  hypothesize the failure mode → test cheaply → read the result → revise. Everyone around him
  works magic by rote on a pre-modern picture of the world; Kaiser reasons each effect from real
  physics and wrings efficiency out of it they can't. He *casts* like they do — but understanding
  is always upstream of the power. (This is the whole point of the
  [Engineering Doctrine](engineering-doctrine.md).) He has no mentor and never did: he built the
  loop himself, alone, on machines nobody else would bother to fix. It is the only thing that
  survives the Mist.
- **Flaw (for depth).** He trusts systems over people. He can read a machine in seconds
  but misreads humans — Anakara taught him to assume bad faith, and it costs him. His
  growth is learning that people are not badly-written code to route around. Nothing has
  challenged this yet; whoever eventually does is unwritten.
- **Hidden canon (Kaiser does NOT know this; reveal is much later).** Kaiser is actually
  an **Ocak Kanlı / Hearth-Blooded** — his body *channels* mana from the Mist on its own, a passive,
  standing regeneration **almost no one else has**: the Ocak Kanlı are a scant handful in all the world,
  born once in a great while into any people, and Kaiser is one. He has no idea. Keep it subtextual. It quietly
  explains why his compressed spark *ignited* instead of dissolving in the Mist (Ch2), and it
  is the eventual key to a self-renewing mana supply that lets him outlast people who must
  wait to refill. Do not tip this before he earns it.

## Arc 1 cast (old world)

Nobody. Arc 1 has no supporting character and no relationship at its centre — that is the design.
Kaiser has no mentor, works alone, is valued by no one, and dies alone. Do not add an old-world
attachment; the arc runs on worth, not on a bond.

## New world cast

**Only one person exists so far, and Kaiser has not met them.**

### The watcher
Unnamed, unseen, unsexed. Someone in the trees who watched Kaiser strip a lantern off the wrecked
caravan and take its crystal (Ch5). Kaiser suspects the presence but never confirms it. Everything
else about them is unwritten, including whether they are hostile, which side of the raid they were
on, and who they will tell.

No other new-world character is established. Earlier drafts carried a roster of unwritten characters
built around a bond-driven plan that has been cut; it was deleted rather than left to steer a story
it no longer fits. Add people when a written chapter needs them, and give each one the fields in the
belief ledger below.

## Belief ledger

For every recurring character, track four separate fields in arc notes or chapter comments:

| Field | Question |
|---|---|
| Evidence | What have they personally seen or verified? |
| Model | What do they think explains it? |
| Public account | What version have they told or recorded? |
| Commitment | What have they risked because of that version? |

Do not synchronize beliefs off-page. Information travels through a speaker, document, price,
rumor, order, or witnessed act. A character may revise their private model while preserving the
public account to protect rank or avoid blame.

Kaiser's recurring blind spot belongs in this ledger: he often identifies what a person is doing
but mistakes why they are doing it. His diagnostic refrain becomes morally complete only when he
learns that human motive cannot always be isolated with a cheap test.
