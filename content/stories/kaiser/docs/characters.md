# Characters

## Kaiser — protagonist

- **Who he is.** A real engineer, not a movie hacker. Credentialed-adjacent skill wasted
  by the system. Maker, not fighter — his instrument is a tool, not a weapon.
- **His actual superpower — method over knowledge.** His old-world electronics knowledge
  mostly does *not* transfer (different physics). What transfers is the **diagnostic
  loop**: observe → hypothesize the failure mode → test cheaply → read the result →
  revise. He treats the unknown methodically while everyone around him treats it as
  superstition. Power is always downstream of understanding; he never "casts," he
  *characterizes a component* and then exploits its operating envelope. (This is the whole
  point of the [Engineering Doctrine](engineering-doctrine.md).) Its origin is now canon: old
  **Izzet** drilled this loop into him in the shop, years before the crystal world (Ch2).
- **Flaw (for depth).** He trusts systems over people. He can read a machine in seconds
  but misreads humans — Anakara taught him to assume bad faith, and it costs him. His
  growth is learning that people are not badly-written code to route around. **Elin**
  cracks this open; **Ostragrad's** genuine fairness confuses him.
- **Hidden canon (Kaiser does NOT know this; reveal is much later).** Kaiser is actually
  an **Ocak Kanlı / Hearth-Blooded** — his body can channel mana directly. He has no
  idea. Keep it subtextual. It quietly explains why his compressed spark *ignited*
  instead of dissolving in the Mist (Ch4), and it is the eventual key to charging crystals
  from his own body. Do not tip this before he earns it.

## Arc 1 cast (old world)

### Izzet
Grumpy, exacting old master mechanic who runs the scrap-and-repair shop below Kaiser's flat.
Years ago he took a raw, undisciplined Kaiser under his wing and drilled the diagnostic method
into him — *"What is it doing? Not what you think — what is it actually doing?"* — so Kaiser's
whole survival skillset in the crystal world is, at root, Izzet's. He is the one person who
ever valued Kaiser and treated him as a person, not a statistic; his harshness at the bench and
his quiet generosity (the birthday meal, the coat, the heater left on) are the same care
wearing two faces (Ch2). Too proud to take help or even name the trouble, he keeps the shop open
— and a lit place for the boy to come back to — by borrowing from loan sharks, and means to
settle it himself; he never tells Kaiser, who only senses something is wrong and lets it go. When
the debt comes due they beat him down, and it is seeing Izzet hurt that drives the non-violent
Kaiser to his one act of agency, which gets Kaiser killed (Ch3). The relationship must carry real warmth — it is the only place Kaiser is
*seen*, and the whole emotional engine of Arc 1.

## On deck (established canon, enters Arc 2+)

### Elin
Orange-haired girl (~12) Kaiser saves in the forest and escorts to Ostragrad; speaks a
border dialect (Russian-adjacent), broken common tongue. Secretly **Lord Valerius's
daughter**. The human relationship that breaks Kaiser's system-over-people flaw.

### Lord Valerius
Elin's father; lord of Ostragrad, powerful northern noble. Spares Kaiser's life but does
not clear him; confines him to the city.

### Commander Vorn
Ostragrad garrison commander; experienced, calm, careful.

### Master smith — Boran / "Kazgrim Steelhand"
Hearth-Blooded iron-and-circuit master; Kaiser is assigned to his workshop as a watched
heavy laborer. **Naming note:** the old `characters.md` listed *Kazgrim Steelhand* —
treated here as the reimagined English-facing name for the Boran role. **Confirm before
it ships.**

### Captain Lyra
One of Valerius's commanders; distrusts Kaiser; armor carries small physical-boost
Enhancer cores.

### Toma
Arrogant journeyman in the workshop.

### Sena
Young soldier assigned to orient Kaiser in the city.
