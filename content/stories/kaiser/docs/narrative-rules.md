# Narrative Rules

Story-specific rules for Kaiser. Line-level language lives in `.claude/CLAUDE.md`; the detailed
craft rationale lives in [Voice Study](voice-study.md). When a rule fights clarity, clarity wins.

## Narrative promise

The reader is here for three linked pleasures:

1. watching a disciplined mind turn observation into leverage;
2. watching different people interpret the same evidence differently;
3. watching every useful solution create a social, moral, or political cost.

The story is not a frictionless power fantasy. Knowledge gives Kaiser an edge, never immunity.

## Point of view

- Default to close third through Kaiser. Narration uses what he can sense, infer, remember, and
  misunderstand now.
- Use other viewpoints when they reveal Kaiser's external effect, institutional consequence, or a
  danger he cannot witness. One scene, one governing consciousness.
- Anchor a viewpoint cut immediately with a want and a lens-specific detail.
- Do not repeat an event merely to show another reaction. Continue from its consequence.
- Save omniscient cutaways for rare dramatic irony. A watcher may be shown; its full explanation may not.

## Reveal discipline

- The reader learns the power system only as fast as a viewpoint earns the observation. The full truth
  remains in [Power System](power-system.md).
- Never name a mechanism before evidence distinguishes it from competing explanations.
- Diegetic foreshadowing is encouraged: an object, price, custom, joke, failure, or ritual may appear
  before anyone understands it.
- A clue must remain innocent on first contact and become sharper in retrospect.
- The reader may know more than Kaiser, but not everything. Prefer one extra implication to a full answer.

## Scene engine

Every substantial scene needs:

- an immediate want;
- a working model of the situation;
- a limit or clock;
- new evidence that breaks one assumption;
- residue: cost, clue, debt, obligation, reputation, or false belief.

A scene that returns everyone to the state in which they entered is probably disposable.

## Competence

- Show skill through procedure and choice. The narrator never labels Kaiser brilliant.
- Kaiser's diagnostic loop is observable: inspect → hypothesize → cheap test → result → revision → act.
- Let him keep more than one hypothesis alive until a test separates them.
- He is allowed to be wrong. His strength is revision without ego.
- Modern physics transfers; local language, law, biology, etiquette, and human motive do not.
- When Kaiser wins technically, ask what attention, dependency, or fear the win purchases.

## Misunderstanding and dramatic irony

- Wrong interpretations must be reasonable from local evidence.
- A misunderstanding becomes plot only when someone commits resources, status, or violence to it.
- Do not clear up every false belief. Track who believes which version in [Characters](characters.md)
  or the arc notes.
- Public composure may hide private uncertainty. Use this gap for both tension and dry humor.
- Never make a whole culture stupid so Kaiser can look clever. Give locals expertise he lacks.

## Power and scale

- Establish the observer's credibility before using their reaction to measure power.
- Prefer controlled power to noise: preparation, restraint, routine, and the ability to leave an opening
  undefended because it is not truly an opening.
- Early Kaiser has leverage, not force. His body, mana, rank, money, and information remain limited.
- A stronger opponent should change the decision tree, not merely raise the number of attacks.

## Side characters

- Every recurring person needs a want, pressure, boundary, mistaken belief, and useful competence.
- Let people plan beyond the current chapter. The future makes loss expensive.
- Antagonists have incentives and procedures. Bureaucratic cruelty is more important than sadism.
- Give ordinary people enough dignity and intelligence that their survival matters independently of Kaiser.
- Warmth should be concrete: shared work, food, correction, a favor disguised as routine.

## Tension and violence

- Run two clocks where possible: physical, resource, social, knowledge, or moral.
- Before violence, establish objective, terrain, false assumption, protected resource, and retreat condition.
- During violence, follow decisions and reversals. Do not catalogue choreography.
- After violence, count the cost in wounds, mana, equipment, witnesses, law, and belief.
- Cost persists across chapters. Competence buys time; it does not erase consequence.

## Worldbuilding and exposition

- Deliver lore through use: price, repair, ritual, work, law, insult, meal, checkpoint, or failure.
- Technical explanation begins with a symptom and ends with a decision.
- Quantities belong only when the stakes need them. Translate the decisive number into time, distance,
  heat, hunger, injury, money, or risk.
- A professional disagreement is better than a neutral lecture.
- Use established terms from [Glossary](glossary.md); do not coin synonyms for variety.

## Tone

- Adult, grounded, restrained. Awe is welcome; wish fulfilment is not.
- The base register is plain and observant. Grandeur comes from consequence, not ornament.
- Emotion appears through body, choice, evasion, and changed behavior. Name it only when ambiguity would
  be artificial.
- Humor comes from incompatible priorities, practical concerns inside ceremony, and Kaiser's dry attempt
  to manage fear. Never turn injury into slapstick.
- The Mist is indifferent, not malicious. Institutions may be cruel without enjoying cruelty.

## Chapter movement

- Open on a task already in progress. Routine gives the first anomaly contrast.
- Alternate pressure with brief ordinary life. A quiet beat must build attachment, plant a rule, or change
  a relationship.
- End after meaning changes: the safe model fails, a witness appears, a solution creates a larger owner,
  or two people leave with incompatible accounts.
- The final line sharpens the next question without announcing a mystery.
- Braid domestic, operational, and strategic scales. After a large turn, return to one ordinary
  object, need, or relationship that now means something different.
- Quiet scenes must perform work: build attachment, reveal hierarchy through routine, plant a usable
  rule, or create a future commitment.
- Prefer recontextualization to simple inflation. A later chapter should often change what an earlier
  gift, rescue, repair, or threat meant.

## Reputation and institutions

- Track three versions of important events: what happened, what Kaiser believes happened, and what
  the institution records.
- A private misunderstanding escalates only through a visible chain: witness, classification,
  superior decision, procedure, consequence.
- Kaiser's public image and private uncertainty may diverge. The image must create both leverage and
  obligation; it cannot remain a consequence-free joke.
- Institutional antagonists protect order, monopoly, or liability. Their calm procedure should be
  more dangerous than theatrical malice.

## Refrains and payoff

- Repeat only what gains meaning.
- Kaiser's own question — *What is it doing? Not what you think; what is it actually doing?* — progresses
  from a habit he taught himself over broken machines, to the one thing the Mist leaves him, to a moral
  challenge.
- Objects and rules should return as mechanisms, not decoration.
- A solution planted early should pay off under a changed cost, never in the exact form first imagined.

## Language and continuity

- Published chapters are written directly in Turkish. Docs remain in English.
- No em dash in Turkish prose. Use native word order and natural Turkish punctuation.
- Common speech is Turkish, but origin, rank, and profession shape vocabulary and rhythm.
- Track who knows what, when they learned it, what they believe instead, and what they have told others.
- When revising a chapter, recheck timeline, wounds, inventory, money, terminology, weather, and active
  misunderstandings in adjacent chapters.
