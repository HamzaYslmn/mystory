# Narrative Rules

General guidelines for the Kaiser story — guidance, not law. The line-level style rules (plain,
flowing, readable, show-don't-tell) live in `.claude/CLAUDE.md`; this page covers the
story-specific things. When any guideline fights readability, readability wins.

## Reveal discipline

- The reader learns the crystal system only as fast as Kaiser can observe and deduce it. The
  [Magic System](magic-system.md) page holds the full truth; each scene shows a sliver, and the
  reader shouldn't get certainty ahead of Kaiser.
- Don't pre-explain a mechanic before the scene that earns it — no forward hints, no early
  diagnostic language for something not yet discovered.

## Tone & voice

- Seinen: adult, grounded, restrained. Awe is fine; wish-fulfillment isn't.
- Convey feeling through action and concrete detail rather than naming it. Earn the emotional
  beat, then don't linger to milk it.
- The system is bureaucratically cruel, not cartoonish — antagonists have reasons (the enforcer
  skimming rations is cold and tired too). Prefer moral weight to good-vs-evil.
- Cost is real and lingers: wounds, debts, and choices carry across chapters. Competence buys
  time, not escape.
- Give Kaiser a dry engineer's humor — levity that makes him human, never slapstick.

## Keeping the reader

- Hook early, and end each chapter on a pull — a question, a reveal, or a want the reader aches
  to see answered.
- The core payoff is the inversion: the engineering method the old world called worthless becomes
  his one real power. Let the reader feel the powerlessness first, then feel the win.
- Deliver the world through Kaiser's eye and dry asides, not exposition blocks. Keep the crystals
  and this world as open questions, paid off on a delay.

## Language

- Published chapters are Turkish; these docs are English drafts. The Turkish must read like native
  Turkish, not a translation.
- The common tongue is Turkish (a plot mechanic), but nobody speaks it identically — a speaker's
  origin leaks through (Elin's mother tongue is Russian-adjacent).
- Reuse established terms instead of coining new words for the same thing, and keep word choices
  consistent from chapter to chapter.
