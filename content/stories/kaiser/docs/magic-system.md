## The crystal ("core" / *çekirdek*)

- Common form: a flawless, near-indestructible **pale-blue hexagonal prism**. Inside the
  glassy body, **runic / geometric lines of light** turn slowly; when active, that light
  visibly comes alive.
- **Blue body = the universal substrate** (the "silicon"). **The colored runes inside =
  the etched function** (the "circuit / firmware"). The *rune color names the family.* So
  the big caravan crystal is *a blue body with red runes* = an Enhancer. Same substrate
  everywhere; different internal rune-light = different job.

## States (charge is separate from structural integrity — two axes)

- **Active:** bright, internal light moving.
- **Weak / dormant (*zayıf*):** dimmed, inner motion slowed — *discharged but sound.
  Recoverable.*
- **Dead / black (*siyah / ölü*):** energy gone, lattice failed, brittle — *scrap.*
- **Dim ≠ dead.** Charge and structural integrity are separate axes — a dim core can be sound.

## The five families (identified by rune color)

Each crystal belongs to **exactly one family and one sub-class within it.** The family
gives the general operating logic; the sub-class fixes the exact job. An Enhancer is not a
general "makes-things-stronger" stone — it magnifies only the one attribute its sub-class
defines. **One crystal, one job. Never two.**

### 1. Enhancer / Booster (*Güçlendirici*) — red runes
Multiplies an existing property of the object / process it is bound to: speed, hardness,
durability, RPM. *Example:* the large caravan crystal is an RPM-boosting Enhancer — if a
wheel normally turns ten times, it drives the connected mechanism to a far higher rate. A
different Enhancer might raise hardness; the same stone does not do both.

### 2. Converter / Transformer (*Dönüştürücü*) — purple runes
Changes one form of energy / property into another: mana→electricity, light→mana,
water→steam. Sub-class-bound. *Example:* the **slime core** is the sub-class that turns
water (or a suitable medium) into acid; with no medium to convert, an active converter can
burn its **own stored mana** into acid instead. Source-tuned converters likewise fall back
to consuming their own reserve when no external source exists.

### 3. Soul Binder / Conjuration (*Ruh Bağlayıcı*) — green runes
Compensates for a damaged or missing part. If suitable raw material is present it weaves it
in; if not, it spins a **temporary substitute from its own energy.**

### 4. Manipulation (*Manipülasyon*) — yellow runes
Automation, binding, unbinding, mechanism control. Issues **limited commands** to an object
/ system; the command type is set by sub-class. **Command cost scales with complexity** —
simple, repetitive tasks are far more sustainable than complex ones. Large storage
sub-classes run city infrastructure (e.g. regularly charging a streetlamp network).

### 5. Special / Legendary (*Özel*) — multi-colored
Do not fit one fixed family. Extremely rare, can exhibit multiple behaviors. No single
color.

## Mana — the power budget

- Crystals **store only mana** — never electricity, flame, heat, or light directly. Mana is
  the one global resource; the right sub-class converts it into acid, light, motion, heat,
  or another output.
- **Blue liquid / aura (*mavi sıvı*):** mana-charged biological conductor in living
  creatures' veins. Crystals **absorb this aura to charge** — even a little blood visibly
  powers a small core.
- **Crystals do NOT self-charge.** Recharging requires a rare Converter that turns a source
  into mana (workshops use dedicated **charge stones / mana-converter stones** — ultra-rare,
  expensive). **Ocak Kanlı** can push mana straight from their own body into a core once
  they have enough control.
- **Rarity tiers** (common / rare / epic / legendary) differ by size, mana capacity, color
  shift (epic trends purple), and *efficiency* — legendary stones do the same job on less
  mana.

## Binding & mechanics (the engineering constraints — this is where scenes live)

- **Binding is done only with Manipulation.** You strike the object with a
  Manipulation-tipped tool to bind a core to it. A bare core usually does little alone; it
  gains its function when bound to an object.
- **The bind line is also a control bridge.** Once bound, you needn't touch the core —
  touching the object's outer surface (e.g. a lamp body) passes the on/off request to the
  core.
- **On/off is a switch, not a program.** Press a palm to the core (or the bound object's
  surface) and feed a little mana to toggle it. An activated core keeps running its **last
  command on its own internal mana** until it gets a cancel signal, even if the external
  mana flow stops.
- **Socket clearance must be left (*yuva payı*).** A working core can flex its inner mana
  line and show tiny thermal expansion. If a metal socket leaves no clearance, the core
  develops **hairline cracks** or the weak metal tab snaps.
- **A core is damaged only as much as its host.** Alone it is tough; but if the part it is
  bound to breaks, the break can crack the core.
- **Removal is clean or destructive.** Clean = Manipulation. Destructive = shatter the host
  to extract, risking the core.
- **Bind marks are permanent.** Binding leaves thin blue vein-like traces on the host;
  removing or killing the core does not fully retract them.
- **Chaining is exponential.** Multiple cores on one mechanism raise power draw
  exponentially — multi-core rigs are only ever short-term or special-purpose.

## Society & craft

- **Ocak Kanlı / Hearth-Blooded:** rare people with extraordinary mana capacity who
  interact with crystal tech directly and can charge cores from their own body.
- **Ordinary people** can carry enough mana to toggle a core but tire under heavy use.
- **Manipulator tools:** small-crystal-tipped tools that *lend* a stone's ability to
  ordinary hands (bind / unbind / probe / limited command) — how non-Hearth-Blooded do
  crystal work at all.
- **Crystal literacy is cargo-cult.** Most local masters have not cracked runes as a
  theory; they remember *shapes that work.* Border settlements pass knowledge
  master-to-apprentice; the capital academies hold the real theory. **This gap is exactly
  the space Kaiser's method exploits.**
- **The world's common tongue is Turkish** (plot mechanic — Kaiser realizes this in
  Freygard). Border villages keep their own tongues (Elin's is Russian-adjacent). Nobody
  speaks the common tongue perfectly and identically — origin leaks through.
